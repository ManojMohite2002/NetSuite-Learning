# Week 10 – Item Governance & Risk Control

## Overview

This week focuses on understanding how **Item Governance & Risk Control** in NetSuite ensures financial accuracy, system integrity, and compliance.

Items are not just operational records. They directly influence:

- Inventory valuation  
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)  
- General Ledger (GL) postings  
- Revenue recognition  
- Profitability reporting  

Because Items act as the bridge between operations and finance, incorrect configuration can introduce significant financial and operational risk.

Item Governance provides a structured framework to:

- Control Item creation and modification  
- Prevent financial misstatements  
- Ensure consistency across transactions  
- Maintain auditability and compliance  

Without proper governance, even small configuration errors can propagate across the system and impact financial reporting.

## Architectural Concept

Item Master Configuration
→ Transaction Processing
→ Item-Level Financial Impact
→ Costing & Valuation Logic
→ GL Posting
→ Financial Reporting

Control points exist at every stage to prevent errors from propagating across the system.


## Why This Matters

Understanding Item Governance ensures:

- Financial data remains accurate and reliable  
- Inventory valuation is consistent and controlled  
- GL postings are correct and auditable  
- System behavior aligns with business rules  
- Risks are identified and mitigated proactively  

A single misconfigured Item can:

- Distort financial statements  
- Impact revenue recognition  
- Break inventory valuation  
- Create audit and compliance issues  
- Propagate errors across multiple transactions
